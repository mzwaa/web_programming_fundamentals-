Milan

The fashion and financial capital of Italy

History

Milan, or Milano in Italian, has a long and storied past. It was originally settled by the Celtic Insubres, conquered by the Romans in 222 BC, and has since been ruled by various empires and kingdoms. The city flourished during the Renaissance and later became a hub of commerce and industry in modern Italy. Today, it blends ancient architecture with cutting-edge design and innovation.

Attractions

Milan is home to some of Italy's most iconic landmarks and cultural treasures:

  • Duomo di Milano: The largest church in Italy, known for its stunning Gothic architecture.
  • Santa Maria delle Grazie: The church housing Leonardo da Vinci's famous mural, "The Last Supper."
  • Sforza Castle: A historic fortress with museums and art collections.
  • La Scala Opera House: One of the world’s premier opera venues.
  • Quadrilatero della Moda: Milan’s high-end fashion district featuring luxury designers and boutiques.

Travel Tips

To get the most out of your visit to Milan, consider the following tips:

  • Use the metro and trams to navigate the city efficiently.
  • Visit in spring or fall for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
  • Book tickets for "The Last Supper" well in advance—it sells out quickly.
  • Try local delicacies like risotto alla Milanese and panettone.
  • Many attractions are closed on Mondays, so plan accordingly.
